Transforming Shell Mobility

A Microservices solution

A digital transformation initiative to optimize loyalty offer management and partner integration, converting complex manual processes into a streamlined ecosystem.

Context & Contribution

Shell Mobility, operating across 36 markets and over 44 000 locations, faced significant operational bottlenecks and high costs due to manual processes, legacy systems, and siloed teams in 2023. Shell partnered with DEUS.AI to help addressing these critical problems.

    My contribution:
  • Researched user needs, pain points, and operational constraints
  • Collaborated to define problems, write scenarios, and translate requirements
  • Developed interactive prototypes to validate solutions with stakeholders
  • Designed intuitive and consistent user interfaces for the new platform

Problem Definition & Opportunity

The core problem was Shell's inability to efficiently manage its Loyalty offers and partner integrations, reducing agility and leading to suboptimal user experiences. The process for new offers, for instance, typically consumed 8-12 weeks per offer due to extensive manual steps, physical sign-offs, and legacy system updates. Key pain points included:

  • Manual processes: Error prone interventions in offer creation and partner onboarding.
  • Legacy system limitations: Monolithic architecture impacting agility and scalability.
  • Siloed Operations: Lack of cross-departmental alignment and data visibility.
  • Slow Time-to-market: Inability to rapidly deploy new loyalty offers and onboard partners.

Addressing these problems, particularly the 53.8 million in annual costs from reconciliation errors and missed partner agreements, proves crucial for enhancing Shell's operational efficiency, accelerating market responsiveness, optimizing customer and partner experiences, and ultimately driving sustainable growth within the competitive mobility retail sectors.

Discovery & Key Insights

Technical Research

The fragmented and monolithic nature of Shell's existing systems presented a clear opportunity for transformation. We identified microservices architecture as the optimal solution to address these core problems. This approach allowed for the breakdown of complex, interdependent systems into smaller, independently deployable services, thereby enabling:

  • Enhanced Agility: Faster development and deployment cycles for new features and offers through independent service updates.
  • Improved Scalability: The ability to scale individual services based on demand, optimizing resource utilization and performance.
  • Reduced Interdependencies: Mitigating the risks and delays associated with changes in monolithic systems by isolating functionalities.
  • Greater Innovation: Fostering a more experimental and responsive development environment by allowing teams to work on services autonomously.

User Research

User research was foundational to our discovery process, specifically:

  • Stakeholder interviews to gather qualitative insights into needs, challenges, and workflows.
  • Task analysis that clearly showed numerous bottlenecks and opportunities for automation.
  • Shadowing to provided firsthand insights into inefficiencies and workarounds in various processes.

Key Insights

Our research revealed critical insights that directly informed the technical strategy:

  • Process Automation is Paramount: Manual steps were the biggest impediment to efficiency, directly informing our decision to implement CI/CD pipelines.
  • Data Fragmentation: Disparate systems led to inconsistent data and reconciliation errors, highlighting the need for a robust data platform and unified services.
  • Need for Self-Service: Empowering marketing and business teams with self-service tools would drastically reduce IT dependency.
  • Importance of Role-Based Access: Granular control was crucial for different user groups, necessitating a robust RBAC system.
  • Scalability for Growth: Future growth demanded a horizontally scalable architecture.

Vision & Strategy

Our solution was designed around four core principles to ensure robustness, flexibility, and maximum value:

  • Decoupled Architecture: By treating frontend and backend modules as separate building blocks, we achieved reusability, faster development, consistent user experience, and flexible API-first logic.
  • Single Customer View: By providing an end-to-end customer and partner view, enabling new insights, informed Business decisions, and optimized resource scaling.
  • Cost Efficiency & Speed: By simplifying processes, reducing partner registration time, loyalty offer assignment errors, and accelerating time to market, while cutting server costs.
  • Future-Proof Solutions: By designing an agnostic solution that adapts seamlessly to any data, platform, or cloud, ensuring long-term scalability, easy reconfigurability, and robust communication between distributed systems.

Challenges & Risks

Implementing the microservices platform presented significant challenges:

  • Integration Complexity: Integrating with existing legacy monolithic systems.
  • Organizational Change Management: Addressing resistance within a large enterprise.
  • Data Consistency: Ensuring accuracy across disparate systems during migration.
  • Security & Compliance: Maintaining robust standards for sensitive data.

These risks were proactively managed through iterative development and continuous stakeholder engagement.

Delivery & Execution

Our delivery was conducted in an agile Scrum environment, driven by a dynamic and responsive roadmap guided by continuous feedback and integration. This iterative approach was underpinned by robust cross-functional team collaboration, fostering shared ownership and seamless translation of business requirements into technical specifications.

Quality assurance was integrated throughout the lifecycle, averaging automated testing and continuous monitoring to ensure high standards and inform ongoing improvements. User Acceptance Testing (UAT) with business users validated features against operational needs.

Technical Architecture

The solution is built on a robust and scalable microservices ecosystem, designed for high performance and flexibility. This architecture was strategically chosen to support high scalability and rapid feature iteration, leveraging an API Gateway for external communication and gRPC for efficient internal service interactions, all supported by a comprehensive DevOps and CI/CD pipeline.

A critical component within this platform is the Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) system which manages user permissions and ensures secure, controlled access across the Shell Retail platform by defining distinct roles with granular permissions and providing clear flow for role creation and assignment.

UX/UI Design

The design process was deeply integrated into agile sprints, focusing on rapid prototyping, iterative user testing, and high-fidelity design. This ensured user feedback continuously refined the product and aligned with product goals, resulting in intuitive and efficient user experiences.

Key Takeaways

Reflecting on the lessons learned throughout the Shell Retail project:

Microservices Drive Agility

Adopting a microservices architecture fundamentally transformed Shell's ability to innovate, integrate, and scale through modularity and independent deployment.

Metrics Validate Impact

Quantifying achievements with clear metrics (e.g., 99% reduction in onboarding time, 3x faster development) was essential in demonstrating tangible value and securing investment.

Cross-Functional Collaboration is Essential

The success of this complex project was a testament to seamless collaboration within our interdisciplinary Scrum team, overcoming enterprise challenges.

Continuous improvement is Key

The data informed approach, coupled with continuous monitoring and feedback, ensures the platform evolves and delivers increasing value.
